Credit Risk Analysis (SAMPLE PROJECT)

INSIGHT

Credit risk assessment is a major challenge for financial institutions, such as credit unions and banks, when extending personal loans to consumers. Predicting a customer's likelihood of defaulting on a loanthat is, of not making the required repaymentsis one of the primary goals. Financial institutions' stability and profitability can be negatively impacted by loan defaults, which can result in large losses. Development of strong predictive models capable of identifying possible defaulters prior to credit issuance is crucial in order to reduce these risks. Numerous types of financial, demographic, and past customer data are used in these models.

AIM & OBJECTIVE

  • Develop a predictive credit risk model
  • Predicting the loan status based on the provided features
  • Creation of a machine learning model that can predict the certainity and uncertainity of customer loan defaulting based on the information available in the dataset.

DATASET

The dataset used in the following project is a public dataset available from kaggle, the dataset is titles as Credit Risk Dataset. The following dataset simulates columns containing Credit Bureau Data.

The following columns are included in the dataset:

  • person_age : Age of the person
  • person_income : Annual income
  • person_home_ownership : Type of residential ownership - rent, own, mortgage etc.
  • peron_emp_length : Employment lenghth in years
  • loan_intent : Loan intent, further classified into 'PERSONAL', 'EDUCATIONAL', 'MEDICAL' etc.
  • loan_grade : Loan grade, further calssified into 'A', 'B', 'C', 'D' and 'E'.
  • loan_amount : Loan amount
  • loan_int_rate : Loan interest rate
  • loan_status : Loan status, '0' for non-default and '1' for default
  • loan_percent_income : Percetage of earnings allocated the loan
  • cb_person_defautl_on_file : Previous history on loan default, 'Y' for yes and 'N' for no
  • cb_person_cred_hist_length : Credit history of the personeel in years

APPROACH

1. Data Exploration

  • Analysis of variable distribution.
  • identifying null/missing and inconsitency in the data.
  • Recognise how the independent and dependent variables are related to one another.

2. Data Preprocessing

  • Handle missing values.
  • Encode categorical variables.
  • Normalize/standardize numerical variables.

3. Data Splitting

  • split data into training & testing datasets.

4. modelling

  • Training data using appropriate machine learning algorithms.
  • Train multiple models and tune hyperparameters.

5. Model Evaluation

  • Implement AUC-ROC, precision recall and F1 score to evaluate models perfomance.

6. Interpretting Results

  • Identify the essential variables for predicting credit risk.
  • Analyze and evaluate the patterns & insights obtained from the model.

7. Implementation

  • Provide a workable plan for incorporating the predictive model into the credit-granting procedures of the financial institution.

GOALS

  • Prior to extending credit, identify high-risk clients in order to lower the default rate.
  • Enhance credit risk assessment accuracy and efficiency.
  • Offer insightful information that can be applied to enhance credit strategies and policies.

CHALLENGES

  • Guarding against false positives (identifying a good customer as bad) and false negatives (identifying a bad customer as good) by maintaining model accuracy.
  • Preserving impartiality and steering clear of prejudices in the model to guarantee that every customer class is handled fairly.
  • Successfully incorporating the model into the financial institution's current systems.
  • In order to mitigate risks and make well-informed decisions when granting loans, credit institutions must develop a strong credit risk prediction model.
